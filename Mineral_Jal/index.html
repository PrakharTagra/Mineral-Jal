Why TDS matters

Ghaziabad's groundwater is hard — here's what four stages of filtering actually remove.

Municipal and borewell water in this belt commonly tests between 400–650 ppm TDS, well above the 300 ppm the Bureau of Indian Standards treats as the acceptable upper limit. Our multi-stage setup brings that down to a safe, mineral-retained range — not zero, since water stripped of all minerals isn't what you want to drink either.

01

Sediment filter

Removes sand, rust and visible particles before they reach the membrane.

02

Carbon block

Strips chlorine, odour and organic contaminants.

03

RO membrane

Forces water through a semi-permeable layer, rejecting dissolved salts and heavy metals.

04

Mineral + UV cartridge

Reintroduces calcium and magnesium for taste, then UV neutralises any remaining microbes.
